Healthcare

Video-Based AI-Generated Content (AIGC) is transforming the healthcare industry by providing unprecedented insights and enhancing patient care. Utilizing artificial intelligence and advanced video analytics, AIGC offers a wealth of applications ranging from remote patient monitoring to surgical training and performance assessment. Its ability to transform video data into granular analytical insights empowers healthcare professionals to make more informed decisions, enhance workflows, and ultimately improve patient outcomes. However, challenges related to data privacy, system interoperability, and ensuring consistent quality in varying clinical settings must be navigated for successful implementation. Regardless, the advent of video-based AIGC presents a significant opportunity for healthcare providers and stakeholders to revolutionize patient care and elevate the standard of health services.

Major Uses In Industry

Surgical Training & Planning

Generative AI models utilize GANs to produce realistic surgical video simulations for training and preoperative planning. These generated images can also serve as a safe and scalable training resource for medical students and healthcare professionals, facilitating learning across various medical conditions and cases.

Mental Health Assessment

AI can diagnose mental health conditions by analyzing facial expressions, speech, and gestures. Emotion recognition models with CNNs and natural language processing aid in diagnosis. AI also generates therapy images and simulations for disorders like phobias and PTSD.

Patient Monitoring & Telemedicine

Real-time video analysis can be used to monitor patients in a hospital setting, especially in ICU or eldercare situations. By tracking movement, facial expressions, and even vital signs, AI can alert healthcare staff about potential emergencies.

Features and Methods of AIGC application

Semantic segmentation:

CNN model with atrous convolutions for frame-level semantic segmentation of the left ventricle. The technique of atrous convolutions enables the model to capture larger patterns and has been previously shown to perform well on non-medical imaging datasets.

Spatiotemporal convolution

a 3-dimensional (3D) convolutional neural network (CNN) with residual connections predicts clip level ejection fraction which incorporate spatial information in two dimensions as well as temporal information in the third dimension(3D)

Surgical phase recognition

Surgical automated phase recognition is an application of machine learning, specifically neural networks, for analyzing intraoperative video data to recognize different phases or steps of a surgery. The mechanism relies on the fact that most surgical procedures include Data Collection, Training(CNN/RNN), and Testing & Validation. Surgical phase recognition has been most commonly used in Surgical Education and Training, Real-Time Surgery Monitoring, and Abnormality Detection.

Instrument recognition

Instrument recognition is an application of computer vision in video analytics that involves the identification and tracking of surgical tools in surgical procedures, offering contextual cues to the surgical phase and aiding in the analysis of procedural complexities and surgeon proficiency. This technology, demonstrated to be feasible in various surgical fields such as laparoscopic gastrectomy and colorectal surgery, has implications for healthcare, including enhancing surgical training and competency assessment, and providing valuable insights for surgical procedure analysis.

Gestures and error recognition

It is an advanced aspect of computer vision, which takes surgical phase and instrument recognition further by analyzing and classifying specific surgical actions or gestures. This mechanism, utilizing deep learning algorithms, can identify and distinguish between different classifications of suturing gestures, for instance, and can even stratify surgical skill levels, offering the potential for automated surgical skill assessments and thus enhancing the quality of healthcare delivery and surgical training.

Anatomical landmark recognition

This is a recent innovation in machine learning for surgery, enabling the identification of surgical anatomy such as safe and unsafe zones during laparoscopic cholecystectomy procedures. This technology, based on deep learning models, can dynamically overlay identified zones onto surgical videos, providing intra-operative augmented reality feedback to surgeons and trainees, thereby facilitating real-time decision support and significantly enhancing patient safety.

Opportunities in Healthcare

AI-Guided Surgery:

AI can revolutionize surgery by providing real-time assistance during surgical procedures. It can recognize surgical instruments and anatomical landmarks, predict the next step in the surgery, and detect potential errors or complications, thereby improving patient safety and surgical outcomes. Furthermore, AI algorithms can analyze a surgeon's performance for training and improvement purposes. They can also provide a dynamic overlay of identified zones onto surgical videos, offering intra-operative augmented reality feedback to surgeons and trainees.

Virtual Reality Training:

AI can be combined with VR to create immersive, realistic surgical training environments. These systems can provide real-time feedback to trainees, helping them improve their surgical skills without risking patient safety. Furthermore, they can be used to assess the competency of surgeons before they operate on real patients. VR training can also be customized to replicate rare or complex surgical cases, giving trainees the opportunity to gain experience in these situations.

Telemedicine and Remote Training:

AI can help overcome geographical barriers in surgical training and care. Surgeons in remote areas can access high-quality training materials and perform complex procedures with AI assistance.

Data Analysis and Research:

With AI's ability to process and analyze large datasets, it could unlock new insights from the vast amount of data generated during surgical procedures. These insights could contribute to medical research and the development of new surgical techniques and treatments.

Challenges in Healthcare

Alteration of doctor-patient communication:

The AI could potentially objectify patients and miss subtle nuances in the communication between patients and doctors.

Dependence on human guidance:

AI systems are not currently ready for independent operation and need lifelong guidance for proper medical application.

Difficulty in detecting conversational cues:

AI may lack the ability to detect conversational cues that could help guide communication to individual levels for the best information delivery.

Technical Limitations and Infrastructure:

Implementing video-based AI models and VR technologies requires advanced technical infrastructure and expertise. This can be a significant barrier, especially in resource-limited settings.

Implementation needs to be gradual and individualized:

The readiness of an AI system using machine learning depends on the specific application and the needs of the user. Factors to consider when determining readiness include safety, robustness, explainability, and ethical considerations.

Challenges in Entertainment Marketplace

Maintaining Authenticity and Originality:

AIGC algorithms face challenges in generating truly original and authentic content. Striking a balance between automated content generation and human creativity is crucial to avoid repetitive or derivative video content.

Ethical Considerations in Deepfakes:

Video-based AIGC raises ethical concerns regarding the creation and potential misuse of deepfake videos. Preventing the malicious use of this technology and safeguarding against misinformation are important challenges to address.

Realistic Rendering and Uncanny Valley:

Achieving realistic rendering in video-based AIGC can be challenging. Algorithms must overcome the "uncanny valley" effect, where generated content appears almost human but lacks naturalness, resulting in a disconcerting user experience.

Legal and Copyright Issues:

The use of AIGC in generating video content may raise legal and copyright concerns. Clear guidelines and regulations need to be established to ensure the proper usage and attribution of generated content, protecting the rights of content creators and avoiding intellectual property disputes.
